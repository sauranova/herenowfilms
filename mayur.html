Mayur Soneji is one of Gujarati cinema's most committed voices — an actor who doesn't simply inhabit roles, but builds entire emotional worlds within them. From the rugged moral complexity of Bhaga Bapu in Victor 303 to the stoic historical grandeur of Jadavbha in Kasoombo, his work is defined by an unflinching authenticity rooted in the culture and landscape of Gujarat.

His path into cinema was never about stardom. It was about stories — the kind that don't get told unless someone refuses to look away. Raised with a deep reverence for Gujarati language, folklore, and community, Mayur brings an insider's understanding to every character, whether a village patriarch, a gangster forged by circumstance, or a father defined by sacrifice.

In 2018, Mayur and his partner Mitali Mayur Soneji founded HereNow Films Pvt Ltd — not just as a production house, but as an institution. The company's Here and Now Film School has become a genuine creative incubator, training the next generation of Gujarati filmmakers with the same rigour and passion that Mayur brings to his own work on screen. Over 225 pieces of content, countless workshops, and a growing community of young filmmakers stand as testament to this mission.

Beyond the screen, Mayur's presence on social media — with 14,000+ followers on @actormayursoneji — reflects an artist who stays deeply connected to his audience, sharing process, craft, and the real texture of creative life in Gujarat.
